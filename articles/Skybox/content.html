Skybox Creation by Photo Fusion

Feb. 2016

Ruoqi He, Chia-Man Hung

What is a Skybox:
To reduce 3D scene complexity, video games often employ skyboxes to present distant sceneries. A skybox is a graphical procedure to represent a 360 degrees spherical panoramic view covering all directions. It consists of six square images forming a cube, such that when viewed from the centre they appear as the entire view of the environment.

We developed methods to construct high-quality skyboxes of real-world scenes from photos using minimal equipments. After taking photos of different directions on a fixed location, we merge them in a seamless way and project them onto the skybox. You can see the result from our Skybox demo - the Grand Hall of Ecole Polytechnique.


Details:

  • Finding pairwise photo homography using feature points extraction (AKAZE) and RANSAC algorithm
  • Established relation between homography and rotation by including a camera-dependant parameter
  • Method 1: Incremental photo-matching with interpolated error compensation
  • Method 2: Global optimization and parameter estimation using Ceres Solver
  • Projection to Skybox - source selection based on Voronoi regions generated by the centers of photos
  • Smooth blending on boundaries of Voronoi regions
